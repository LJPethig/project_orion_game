## The Tempus Fugit

- **Built:** 17-03-2223 at 13:47 — Callisto Orbital Works, an independent shipyard
- **Age at game start:** 53 years — well past its planned operational life
- **Original lease:** 25 years, taken out new by Enso VeilTech in 2223, expired 2248
- **Purchase:** Enso VeilTech purchased at residual value in 2248, intending to run for a further 20-25 years then sell as scrap or in working condition
- **Current status:** Still in service in 2276 — 28 years past purchase, kept running because it remained profitable and Jack was willing to fly it
- **Maintenance:** Maintained to minimum legal standard only. Enso VeilTech used a Preferred Service Partner arrangement — cheapest approved parts, minimum required intervals, signed off by inspectors who knew which side their bread was buttered. Most warranties expired years ago and nobody checked.

---

## Jack Harrow

- **Age:** Mid thirties
- **Employer:** Enso VeilTech — 8 years service
- **Family:** None. Father deceased (small inheritance received). No other family.
- **Finances:** Has been saving throughout his Enso VeilTech tenure. Combined savings plus inheritance put him close to the purchase price of the Tempus Fugit.
- **Purchase agreement:** Entered into a formal sale agreement with Enso VeilTech over a year before game start. Terms: Jack must complete the purchase of the Tempus Fugit no later than **13:00 hrs on 01-10-2276** — approximately 3 weeks from game start.
- **Last run:** This cargo run is Jack's final Enso VeilTech contract. On delivery he receives his last paycheck, which combined with his savings completes the purchase. He was days away from owning his ship outright.

---

## Game Start — 08-09-2276 at 03:16

- **18 days** of travel time remaining to destination
- Jack is in hypersleep. Without the impact event he would have slept until 1 day from docking.
- **3 months before game start** — a bank hack destroys Jack's finances. He is blacklisted. The mainframe is aware. Enso VeilTech corporate is aware.
- **Enso VeilTech's decision:** allow Jack to complete the delivery and detain him on arrival. Cheapest option. No disruption to the cargo run. Security and legal arranged to meet him at the destination.
- **The impact event changes everything.** The mainframe wakes Jack — only he can repair the ship. Enso VeilTech is forced to divert a security vessel at significant expense. That vessel is **48 hours away.**
- All costs will be billed to Jack: full ship purchase price, legal fees, security vessel diversion costs, and anything else Enso VeilTech's legal department can attach to the invoice.
- Jack wakes at 03:16 not knowing any of this. Three messages are waiting.

---

## The 48-Hour Window

Jack has 2 days to:
1. Repair the ship damage caused by the impact event
2. Hack the mainframe and override its autonomous control
3. Override the navigation lock
4. Escape before the security vessel arrives

This is borderline impossible. That is the point.

---

## Enso VeilTech — The Corporation

- Not cartoonishly evil — simply indifferent. Jack is employee number 7,341,892.
- Every aspect of Jack's working life is owned, controlled, and monitored by Enso VeilTech — the ship, the cargo, the mainframe software, the scan tool manuals, the employment contract.
- Their compliance system is automated. The response to the bank hack and blacklisting is swift, impersonal, and devastating. The fact that Jack is alone in deep space with a damaged ship is not a variable their system is designed to handle.
- Enso VeilTech is the invisible antagonist of the entire game. Jack never fights them directly — he simply has to survive in a world they have made very difficult for him to exist in.
